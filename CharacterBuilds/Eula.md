The arrival of Eula, all the way back in v1.5 of the game took a lot of people by storm. Many people on the internet attest to empting their wallets pulling for her - convinced by how "cute" she looked when she sneezed in her character teaser. Yes, you heard it right! Some people were that down-bad for the uncontested 5-star Physical DPS Queen right from her introduction.

And adding fuel to the fire, a lot of people on twitter, reddit, and genshin discord forums all over the world were losing their minds (figuratively) seeing her dance in her trailer, and hoped to get her in their team as soon as possible.

At a first glance, being a cryo user, most people would think that her strength is in cryo damage. But no. The adage "Appearances can be deceiving" is quite apt for Eula's case as she is a strong Physical Carry.

In this guide, we will have a good look into Eula's gameplay, her kit, constellations, teams, weapons, artifacts, and other questions you might have while considering for Eula. So, let's get started!

## General overview of the character

To start off, Eula is a limited 5-star, claymore-wielding physical DPS unit.

That said, despite being a Cryo user, her main strength comes from Physical Damage.

As such, it is quite important to build her as a DPS unit. She can be summarized as a Physical Hyper-Carry on-field damage dealer, requiring her to be on the field for the most part.

Her normal attacks, interwoven with her elemental skill contribute a significant portion of her on-field time.

However, the spotlight of her kit is her elemental burst. Her elemental burst is considered to be one of the biggest damage nukes in the entire game - with a high damage ceiling when done right, and has even bigger damage ceiling with her constellations.

And an interesting point to consider is that her elemental burst deals physical damage - and not cryo damage.

## General gameplay style

When playing Eula, it is necessary to concentrate on her normal attacks and elemental bursts.

So, from a gameplay point of view, it is recommended to plan your team rotations such that your supports and sub-DPS units plant all the buffs (for the team), and debuffs (on the enemies), before letting her take centre stage for a good while.

Being a physical carry, Eula greatly benefits from the superconduct reaction. Although superconduct in and of itself does not do significant damage, the -40% Physical Resistance is quite a significant boost to Eula's damage.

As a natural consequence, having an electro character in the team greatly helps Eula. The electro unit can maintain an electro aura on the enemies, while enabling her to proc the superconduct with her Elemental Skill - that deals cryo damage.

## A Tall Claymore User Advantage

Since she wields a claymore, her normal attacks are considered "blunt" attacks.

In the gameplay mechanics, a "blunt" attack is considered to be a very heavy attack - an attack that deals quite a lot of force.

As a result, her normal attacks can easily break crystal chunks, iron chunks, and other rocky minerals with ease in the overworld. This makes her an ideal candidate for farming ores for your weapon enhancements.

Her blunt attacks are useful in breaking enemy shields. So, if you have a geo enemy with a shield like "Stone-hide Lawachurl", you can definitely use her to break shields. While you can use her attacks to break abyss mage shields (if you are desperate/stuck in a pinch), ideally it is better to use an appropriate elemental reactions for them.

Additionally, her blunt attacks are not ideal for frozen enemies. Although she can keep enemies frozen using her elemental skill when they have a hydro aura on them, her normal attacks proc "Shatter" reaction rendering the enemies mobile - defeating the purpose of freeze reactions in the first place.

Since her model is that of a tall female, she can cover a longer distance while running, sprinting, dashing, or swimming in the overworld or during mid-fights. This comes a tad bit handy in time trials and especially in the absence of fast distance traversing characters like Ayaka, Mona, Sayu, Yelan, Wanderer, etc.,

## Normal attacks

Eula has a 5-hit normal attack combo. Being a dancer, the game devs managed to incorporate her dance moves quite gracefully into her normal attacks.

Her first and second normal attacks come in quick succession.

Her third attack is slightly delayed, but it deals two instances of damage.

Her fourth attack comes without any delay.

Finally, her fifth and final attack comes with a noticeable amount of delay and deals two instances of damage. The final instance of damage hits quite hard with the largest damage multiplier, and can knock back smaller enemies.

Eula's normal attacks are the bread-and-butter of her kit. While it takes a considerable amount of time for her elemental burst to recharage, she needs to use her normal attack to maintain a consistent flurry of damage.

For optimized speed-runs and time-sensitive battles, it is often adviced to do the N4 combo and dash out of it to skip the N5 attack as it takes a noticeable amount of time.

Quick side note: If you stop attacking after her 4th Normal attack, you can actually see Eula smile with a mild blush.

## Charged Attacks

Being a tall female model, she does not spin like a beyblade with her claymore while attacking enemies.

Instead she launches a rapid flurry of attacks in quick succession dealing multiple rounds of physical damage.

Her charge attacks do have strength to break enemy shields, and her finishing blow has the ability to knock smaller enemies and stagger bigger enemies.

However, it is not usually recommeneded to use her charge attacks as it takes some time to get charged-up and all the individual hits are not sufficiently strong.

Besides, it consumes stamina, and it becomes a trouble if you are on low stamina and need to dodge enemy attacks.

## Plunge Attacks

Plunge attacks are quite standard. It doesn't contribute to her damage in a regular combat. However, in some special cases where the terrain is advantageous, or you have jumping boost buffs, her plunge attacks can come in handy.

Under specific set of circumstances, being a tall model character, it helps her access the "Dragonstrike" attack.

If the enemy has a noticeably large hitbox, and you notice a decent amount of hitlag, with enough attack speed, you can Dragonstrike with Eula.

Since plunge attacks have the highest multipliers in the normal attack talent, a potential Dragonstriking Eula will be quite an interesting playstyle.

Sadly, pulling it consistently is quite a pain, as most enemies don't have a wide hitbox and you need a lot of attack speed and movement speed. So, I wouldn't recommend aiming for this.

## Elemental Skill

Her elemental skill has two versions: a tap version and a hold version.

### Tap Version

When you tap her skill, she deals one instance of cryo damage on the enemy. If the attack successfully hits the enemy, then she gains a "Grimheart" stack. Eula can hold upto 2 Grimheart stacks. The tap version has a short cooldown of 4 seconds.

### Hold Version

When you Hold E, she deals a bigger AoE Cryo Damage. If there were any Grimheart stacks, she consumes them in the attack. The hold version has a cooldown of 10 seconds.

Each consumed Grimheart stack:
* Reduces the Physical and Cryo RES of the enemy.
* Deals an additional cryo damage on the enemy.

And once you get her A1 passive, the hold version also delivers a mini-burst dealing physical damage.

So, for better damage output, it is adviced to use her "Hold" version after gathering 2 Grimheart stacks.

The Cryo resistance shred is not a big value addition, but the physical resistance reduction is a big plus for her.

### Grimheart stacks
When Eula gains a Grimheart stack, it increases her interruption to resistance, and also increases her DEF noticeably. As a result, she will be able to tank enemy hits a bit more, and take less damage due to the increase in DEF. The interruption to resistance is a good QoL for her as it does not stagger/knock her out easily.

However, this does not make her invincible. She can still be one-shot killed like other characters by strong enemy attacks. So, it is adviced to dodge or use a shielder, and take the defense boost as a small damage mitigator.

When you get Grimheart stacks, you'll notice a small sword icon above her head, and her cape starts to glow. The sword icon appears for a split second and vanishes. Her cape glowing is considerably noticeable. One sword and a mildly glowing cape means 1 stack. Two swords and a brightly glowing cape means 2 stacks.

Additionally, you'll see snowflake animation all around her for a noticeable period of time.

## Elemental Burst
This is the signature talent of her entire kit. On activating it, she deals a small cryo damage attack and creates a lightfall sword that follows Eula for 7 seconds.

Now, every instance of attack Eula deals to the enemies adds one stack of damage to the to the lightfall sword. Every stack accumulated increases the size of the sword.

Once 7 seconds are up, or if Eula swaps to other characters, the lightfall sword detonates dealing a huge backloaded physical damage.

Total burst damage = Base lightfall sword damage + (number of stacks x damage per stack)

### Factors to consider
* The elemental burst does not snapshot. So, if you use attack boosters like Bennett and it vanishes before the burst explodes, the attack boost is lost.

* Only an on-field Eula can detonate the lightfall sword. If you swap to some other character in the team, the lightfall sword prematurely detonates. This can be used advantageously with experience to defeat enemies without the need for an overkill. Also, this can be used as an emergency counter measure in case Eula is quite low on health and can die with an enemy attack.

* If Eula dies during the charging of the lightfall sword, the lightfall sword vanishes. So, it is absolutely crucial to keep her alive for the entire 7 second duration of the charging period.

* Under normal conditions (without any ATK speed boost) you can stack upto 12-13 stacks easily with optimal attack strings and optimal conditions. With her C6 constellation, she can potentially reach her stack limit of 30 - and at that point - she is arguably the strongest one-shot damage dealer in the entire game's roster.

### Burst Attack strings.

For a C0 Eula with an activated A1 and A4 passives, this is an effective combination of attacks to get the most out of her burst damage.

Press Skill -> Burst -> N4 -> Hold Skill -> NA till sword explodes.


Pressing skill gives one Grimheart stack. Burst does a small damage initiates the lightfall sword, and adds one more Grimheart stack. N4 gives her 5 stacks (3rd NA gives 2 stacks). Hold skill launches an attack, 2 sword attacks, mini-lightfall sword damage  - giving 4 stacks plus physical and cryo resistance shreds. Upto this point - you are guaranteed 9 stacks. Depending on if you can complete the N3 combo, you gain 3-4 stacks leading to a 13 stack burst damage with physical resistance drop on the resistance shredded enemies. Hopefully this crits to get a massive Damage-per-screenshot number.

Alternatively, you have a slightly simpler version of this attack string combination

Press Skill -> Burst -> Hold Skill -> NA till sword explodes.

In this combination, you do the hold skill in advance to prematurely reduce the physical resistance of the enemies, and then continue your normal attack strings. This is a bit easier to execute. For the NA attacks, it is recommended to use N4 combination to pack as many stacks as possible. If you want to take it slightly easier, and don't mind not gaining one or two additional stacks, you can stick with N5 combination.

While there might be slightly sophisticated attack string combinations possible to get more stacks, these two are the fundamental ones. So, it is quite recommended to know these attack strings first before going to a lot refined attack strings.
